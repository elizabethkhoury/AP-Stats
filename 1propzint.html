1-Proportion Z-Interval

Parameter

p = the true proportion of the population

Conditions

  • Random sample taken
  • Population size is at least 10 times the sample size
  • np ≥ 10 and n(1 - p) ≥ 10

Calculate

Example: Chick-fil-A Water Cup Problem

The manager at Chick-fil-A is concerned about customers who ask for a water cup when placing an order but fill it up with a soft drink. The manager selects a random sample of 80 customers who asked for a water cup before placing an order and found that 23 of them filled it up with a soft drink.

Step 1: Procedure

1-Prop Z-Interval

Step 2: Parameter

p = the true proportion of all customers who, having asked for a water cup, fill it up with a soft drink.

Step 3: Conditions

  • Random: The sample was randomly selected.
  • 10% Condition: 80 is less than 10% of all Chick-fil-A customers.
  • Large Counts:
    • np̂ = 80 × 23/80 = 23 ≥ 10
    • n(1 - p̂) = 80 × 57/80 = 57 ≥ 10

Step 4: Calculations

  • p̂ = 23/80 = 0.2875
  • Standard Error (SE) = √[(0.2875)(0.7125)/80] ≈ 0.0509
  • z* = 1.96 for 95% confidence
  • Margin of Error = 1.96 × 0.0509 ≈ 0.0998
  • Confidence Interval: (0.188, 0.387)

Step 5: Conclusion

We are 95% confident that the true proportion of all customers who ask for a water cup and fill it with a soft drink is contained in the interval (0.188, , 0.387).
