2-Proportion Z-Test

Formula

z = (p̂₁ - p̂₂) / √(p̂(1 - p̂)(1/n₁ + 1/n₂))
Where p̂ is the pooled sample proportion.

Interpretation

If the p-value is less than alpha, we reject the null hypothesis. This provides evidence that the proportions are significantly different.

Conditions

  • Random samples
  • Independent groups
  • np ≥ 10 and n(1-p) ≥ 10 for both groups

Example

A school wants to know if the proportion of students who pass a test is different between two teachers' classes. Teacher A: 45 out of 60 passed.
Teacher B: 30 out of 50 passed.
A 2-proportion Z-Test is used to determine if there's a significant difference.
